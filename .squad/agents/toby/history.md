## Learnings

### Session 1: Learning Objectives Framework

**Pedagogical Decisions Made:**

1. **Progressive Responsibility Model:** Each lab shifts agency from the system to the learner:
   - Labs 0–1: Understanding and retrieval (system as tool)
   - Labs 2–3: Control and autonomy (learner as designer)
   - Labs 4–5: Orchestration and governance (learner as architect)
   - Lab 6: Transformation (learner as leader)

2. **"Aha Moment" as Core Design:** Every lab has one clear insight that should change how learners think about AI. These moments are *emotional and conceptual*, not just technical:
   - Lab 0: Value first (before technology)
   - Lab 1: Augmentation not automation (safety insight)
   - Lab 2: Accountability is safety (not capability limits)
   - Lab 3: Scope enables reliability (paradox of less is more)
   - Lab 4: Coordination is harder than creation (systems thinking)
   - Lab 5: Governance enables speed (not a constraint)
   - Lab 6: Invisibility means maturity (not absence)

3. **Mental Models Over Technical Details:** Each lab includes one strong analogy (captain/co-pilot, medical panel, airline safety) that maps AI concepts to human experience. This makes the concepts sticky and transferable.

4. **Failure as Pedagogy:** Built failure points into each lab (RAG returning noise, agent escalating, system drifting) so learners experience why constraints matter. Failure teaches better than success.

5. **Scaffolding Strategy:** Each lab builds on the *trust* established in the previous lab:
   - Lab 1 trusts RAG; Lab 2 asks: what if it's wrong?
   - Lab 2 trusts humans; Lab 3 asks: can we automate safely?
   - Lab 3 trusts agents; Lab 4 asks: what if multiple agents disagree?
   - Lab 4 scales agents; Lab 5 asks: how do we govern scale?
   - Lab 5 establishes governance; Lab 6 asks: what if governance is invisible?

6. **Capstone Understanding (Lab 6):** Lab 6 is not about new technology—it's about *organizational transformation*. By then, learners should see that AI modernization is a business and culture problem, not a technology problem.

7. **Success Metrics as Teaching Tools:** For each lab, success looks like a specific artifact or capability. These metrics are shared with the learner *before* the lab, so they know what "winning" means. This is scaffolding through clarity.
